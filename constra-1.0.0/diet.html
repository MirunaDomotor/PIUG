Diet

Hamsters are delightful, tiny pets known for their energetic and inquisitive nature. To ensure that your hamster remains healthy and happy, providing the right diet is essential.


A mix of specially formulated hamster food

In the wild, hamsters naturally eat a mixture of seeds, cereals, insect larvae and larger insects such as crickets. As pets, hamsters need a balanced and varied diet containing all the essential nutrients and minerals they need. Look for a high-quality hamster mix that includes a variety of seeds, grains, and pellets. These mixes are designed to provide essential nutrients and promote dental health.

Put their food in flat dishes or directly on the cage floor. If in a dish, expect them to turn it over to transfer the contents to their larder. They carry food in their cheek pouches and can be seen retreating from their food with bulging cheeks. They like to sit up and hold pieces of food to gnaw.

Fresh vegetables and fruits

In addition to commercial food, hamsters can enjoy a variety of fresh vegetables and fruits. These should be given in moderation as treats, as hamsters have small digestive systems. Safe options include carrots, broccoli, cucumbers, apples, and small pieces of berries. Make sure to wash and cut them into small, manageable pieces.

Protein sources

Hamsters require protein for their growth and overall well-being. You can offer occasional protein-rich treats like plain cooked chicken or mealworms. However, be cautious not to overdo it, as excessive protein can lead to health issues.

Fresh water

Access to clean, fresh water is crucial for your hamster's health. Provide water in a sipper bottle or a small, shallow dish that is easy for your pet to access. Change the water regularly to prevent contamination.

Wet or powdered food

You should only give your hamster wet or powdered food if you're advised to by your vet, for example because of dental problems. Wet food can be difficult to clean from the cage, and it's susceptible to mould and bacteria growth, which can be harmful to your hamster. If your hamster is sick and needs wet food, it's important to remove all traces of it at least twice a day to ensure the food doesn't start to rot.

Avoid harmful foods

Certain foods are toxic to hamsters and should be avoided entirely. These include chocolate, caffeine, garlic, onions, and citrus fruits. Also, refrain from feeding your hamster any human junk food, sugary treats, or processed foods.

Monitor your hamster's diet

Hamsters are prone to obesity if overfed. Stick to recommended portion sizes, and monitor your hamster's weight regularly. Adjust the diet if needed to maintain a healthy weight. If they start eating less, their droppings become moist or their hind-quarters become soiled, talk to your vet immediately. Avoid sudden dietary changes and stale food, as this can upset their stomach.
